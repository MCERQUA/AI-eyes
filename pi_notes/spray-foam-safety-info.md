SPRAY FOAM SAFETY & HEALTH PROTOCOLS

PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE):
- Respiratory protection: NIOSH-approved respirator with organic vapor cartridges
- Chemical-resistant gloves: Nitrile or neoprene (not latex)
- Protective clothing: Coveralls, chemical-resistant apron, boot covers
- Eye protection: Chemical safety goggles or full-face shield
- Skin protection: Barrier cream exposed skin areas
- Hearing protection: When using application equipment

HAZARDOUS EXPOSURES:
- Inhalation: Isocyanates can cause respiratory sensitization
- Skin contact: Can cause chemical burns, dermatitis, sensitization
- Eye contact: Severe irritation, potential corneal damage
- Chemical reactions: Heat generation during application (200Â°F)
- Uncured foam: Contains unreacted isocyanates and other chemicals
- Off-gassing: Can occur during application and curing process

WORKSITE SAFETY:
- Ventilation: Minimum 10 air changes per hour during application
- Restrict access: Only trained personnel should be present
- Emergency equipment: Eye wash station, safety shower readily available
- Spill containment: Absorbent materials for chemical spills
- Fire safety: No open flames, sparks near application areas
- Temperature control: Maintain optimal working conditions

FIRST AID PROCEDURES:
- Skin contact: Wash immediately with soap and water for 15+ minutes
- Eye contact: Flush with water for 15+ minutes, seek medical attention
- Inhalation: Move to fresh air, seek medical help if breathing difficulties
- Swallowing: Do NOT induce vomiting, seek immediate medical help
- Clothing contamination: Remove contaminated clothing immediately

SAFETY TRAINING REQUIREMENTS:
- OSHA Hazard Communication Standard training
- Product-specific safety data sheet (SDS) review
- Emergency procedures and first aid certification
- Proper PPE selection and usage training
- Chemical handling and storage procedures
